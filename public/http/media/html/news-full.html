Changes in driver document verification since the beginning of the year

Amendments have been made to the Law on the National Police, updating the procedure for checking the documents of vehicle drivers.

Document Verification for Drivers by Police Officers: Updated Requirements and Procedures

Since the beginning of 2024, important changes to the legislation regarding the verification of documents for vehicle drivers have come into force in Ukraine. These amendments to the Law of Ukraine "On the National Police" were introduced to improve road safety, reduce the level of offenses, and enhance the procedure for driver-police interaction during roadside checks.

Main Reasons for Document Verification

Verifying drivers' documents is one of the key functions of the National Police, carried out to maintain public safety and order on the roads. The law stipulates that police officers have the right to stop vehicles and request the presentation of documents in the following cases:

  • Violation of traffic rules (TAC). This is the most common reason for stopping vehicles. If a driver violates established rules, the police officer has the right to demand the presentation of a driver's license, vehicle registration documents, and insurance policies.
  • Suspicion of technical malfunction of the vehicle. If the vehicle appears faulty or poses a danger to other road users, the police officer may stop the vehicle for a technical check and document verification.
  • Conducting special operations. The National Police have the right to check drivers' documents as part of special operations, such as searching for stolen vehicles or criminals.
  • Suspicion of a criminal offense. If the driver or passengers are suspected of being involved in a criminal offense, the police officer may request documents to confirm their identity and the legality of the vehicle use.
  • Alcohol or drug intoxication. If the police officer has grounds to believe that the driver is intoxicated, they may stop the vehicle for document verification and direct the driver for a medical examination.

Documents Drivers Must Present

The law defines a list of documents that the driver is required to carry and present at the request of the police:

  • A driver's license of the appropriate category.
  • A vehicle registration certificate.
  • A mandatory civil liability insurance policy for vehicle owners (MTPL).
  • For commercial vehicle drivers, additional documents confirming the right to carry out transportation are required.

Verification Procedure

According to the updated legal norms, the police officer is required to verify the documents strictly within the scope of their powers and in accordance with established procedures. During the vehicle stop, the police officer must identify themselves, state their position, and explain the reason for the stop. The driver, in turn, has the right to know why they were stopped and may ask for clarification on the reasons for the document check.

The police officer does not have the right to demand documents from the driver without explaining the reason for the stop or in the absence of legal grounds for the check. Additionally, the driver has the right to record the actions of the police officers using video or audio recording, which helps ensure transparency and compliance with the rights of all road users.

Refusal to Present Documents and Its Consequences

Refusal by the driver to present documents to the police is considered an administrative offense, which can have serious consequences. According to the Code of Administrative Offenses of Ukraine, a driver who refuses to present documents at the request of a police officer may be fined or even temporarily deprived of the right to drive the vehicle.

Moreover, attempting to flee from the police or refusing to comply with their lawful demands can lead to more serious legal consequences, including detention or prosecution for resisting law enforcement officers.

Drivers' Rights and Responsibilities During Document Checks

Drivers have several rights during document checks:

  • To know the reason for the stop and the purpose of the check.
  • To request the police officer's identification.
  • To record interactions with police officers using video or audio devices.
  • To challenge the actions of the police if their powers are violated.

At the same time, drivers are required to:

  • Present documents at the request of the police officer.
  • Comply with the lawful demands of law enforcement officers.
  • Maintain public order and not obstruct the police in performing their duties.

New Technologies in Document Verification

With the introduction of digital technologies, the National Police increasingly use electronic means to verify documents. Drivers can present documents in the form of digital copies through the "Diia" app. This significantly simplifies the verification process, as it allows drivers to store all necessary documents electronically and quickly provide them at the police officer's request.

Conclusion

The updated rules for document verification for drivers introduced in Ukraine aim to improve road safety and ensure proper control over compliance with traffic rules. Drivers should be aware of their rights and obligations, as well as comply with the lawful demands of the police to avoid misunderstandings and legal consequences during checks.
